The Philosophy Behind the Name

Cicada 3301 —
The Internet's Greatest Mystery

In January 2012, a cryptic image appeared on 4chan. It contained a hidden message. Solving it led to another puzzle. Then another. The trail spanned number theory, steganography, ancient Mayan numerology, runic ciphers, and physical posters appearing simultaneously in Warsaw, Paris, Seoul, and Dallas — all signed only "3301".

The puzzles were extraordinarily sophisticated — recruiting only the most determined minds through layers of encryption, Tor hidden services, and GPS coordinates to real-world dead drops. Three consecutive years of puzzles. Then silence. No organisation ever claimed authorship.

"The best minds are not in government. If you think you have the skills to help us, come find us." — Cicada 3301, 2012

The name Cicada was chosen for this firm because it embodies a philosophy that shapes everything here. A cicada lives underground for 13 or 17 years — prime numbers, mathematically precise — and emerges only when conditions are exactly right. Patient. Unhurried. Precise.

Cicada 3301 attracted people who refused to stop at the surface. It rewarded relentless curiosity, lateral thinking, and the willingness to find signal in noise. That is the approach applied to every engagement here — in governance, risk, and compliance where the real problem is rarely the visible one.

The mystery is the method. Excellence is not announced. It is found by those who know how to look.
