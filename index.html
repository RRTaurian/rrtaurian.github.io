The Philosophy Behind the Name

Cicada 3301 —
The Internet's Greatest Mystery

In January 2012, a cryptic image appeared on 4chan. It contained a hidden message. Solving it led to another puzzle. Then another. The trail spanned number theory, steganography, ancient Mayan numerology, runic ciphers, and physical posters appearing simultaneously in Warsaw, Paris, Seoul, and Dallas — all signed only "3301".

The puzzles were extraordinarily sophisticated — recruiting only the most determined minds through layers of encryption, Tor hidden services, and GPS coordinates to real-world dead drops. Three consecutive years of puzzles. Then silence. No organisation ever claimed authorship.

"The best minds are not in government. If you think you have the skills to help us, come find us." — Cicada 3301, 2012

The name Cicada was chosen for this firm because it embodies a philosophy that shapes everything here. A cicada lives underground for 13 or 17 years — prime numbers, mathematically precise — and emerges only when conditions are exactly right. Patient. Unhurried. Precise.

Cicada 3301 attracted people who refused to stop at the surface. It rewarded relentless curiosity, lateral thinking, and the willingness to find signal in noise. That is the approach applied to every engagement here — in governance, risk, and compliance where the real problem is rarely the visible one.

The mystery is the method. Excellence is not announced. It is found by those who know how to look.

Cicada GRC Advisory operates as a structured collective of independent practitioners. On Anonymity — A Direct Explanation

Contributors to Cicada GRC do not attach personal names to public-facing materials. This is not an evasion — it is a deliberate operating principle grounded in three practical realities.

First, the nature of engagements. Work in governance, risk, and compliance frequently involves unreleased findings, regulatory correspondence, and material non-public information. Public attribution creates conflict with existing NDAs and client confidentiality obligations.

Second, the structure of the collective. Individual contributors cycle across engagements. A named individual model implies continuity and personal brand that does not reflect how the work is actually structured or delivered.

Third, conflict avoidance. Named individuals in advisory roles attract institutional bias — prior employers, current affiliations, and industry relationships all create perceived or actual conflicts. Anonymity is a structural control against that.

Verified credentials and contributor backgrounds are disclosed under mutual NDA at the start of formal engagement. No client has been asked to take qualifications on faith.

04 — Engagement Model

How We Work

Every engagement follows a consistent operating framework. No ambiguity on scope, retention, or confidentiality.

NDA First

A mutual non-disclosure agreement is executed before any substantive discussion. Scope, credentials, and methodology are disclosed only within that protected context. Clients are not asked to share sensitive information without legal protection in place.

Zero Retention

Client data, findings, and documentation are not retained beyond the engagement period. Deliverables are transferred in full to the client. No proprietary data is used in any other context, including internal knowledge bases or future engagements with third parties.

Independent Advisory

Cicada GRC holds no vendor partnerships, reseller agreements, or referral arrangements with any technology provider. Recommendations are made on technical merit. There is no commercial incentive to steer clients toward any particular platform or product.

Identity Restoration

Step-by-step support for victims of identity theft. We provide guidance on securing compromised accounts, notifying relevant authorities, and cleaning up your digital footprint.

Process

  • Identify which accounts and data were compromised
  • Secure or reset all exposed credentials (email, banking, social media, etc.)
  • File reports with law enforcement (IC3) and consumer protection agencies (FTC in the US, local equivalents elsewhere)
  • Place fraud alerts with credit bureaus
  • Monitor credit reports and bank accounts for unauthorized activity
  • Remove exposed personal data from public databases and data broker sites where possible
